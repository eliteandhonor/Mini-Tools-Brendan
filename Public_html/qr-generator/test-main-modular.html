Frequently Asked Questions

What's the difference between error correction levels?

Error correction levels determine how much damage a QR code can sustain while remaining scannable. Low (7%) is suitable for clean, digital displays. Medium (15%) works well for most printed materials. Quartile (25%) is ideal for outdoor use or environments where damage is likely. High (30%) provides maximum resilience but results in denser, more complex codes.

Which format should I choose for my QR code?

PNG is recommended for most uses as it provides lossless compression and transparency support. JPEG is suitable when file size is a concern, though it uses lossy compression. SVG is ideal for scalable graphics that need to be resized without quality loss, perfect for professional printing and large displays.

How much data can a QR code store?

QR codes can store varying amounts of data depending on the character type: up to 7,089 numeric characters, 4,296 alphanumeric characters, or 2,953 binary bytes. However, for optimal scanning reliability, we recommend keeping content concise. URLs should be shortened when possible, and text should be under 300 characters for best results.

Can I add my company logo to QR codes?

Yes! You can upload any image file (PNG, JPG, GIF up to 5MB) to embed as a logo in your QR codes. Choose from different logo sizes (10%-25% of QR code size) and background styles including transparent, white circle, or rounded square. The logo is placed in the center and the QR code's error correction ensures it remains scannable.
